J.K. Rowling

Author of the Harry Potter series

Joanne Rowling, better known as J.K. Rowling, is a British author and screenwriter. She is best known for writing the Harry Potter series, which has won multiple awards and sold over 500 million copies, becoming the best-selling book series in history. Rowling has also written several other novels, including The Casual Vacancy and The Cuckoo's Calling, under the pseudonym Robert Galbraith.

Rowling was born on July 31, 1965, in Yate, Gloucestershire, England. She studied French and Classics at the University of Exeter and worked as a researcher and bilingual secretary for Amnesty International before becoming a full-time writer. In addition to her writing, Rowling is known for her philanthropic work, supporting multiple charities and founding the Lumos Foundation, which works to help children in institutions around the world.

Notable Awards and Honors

  • Order of the British Empire (2000)
  • French Legion of Honour (2009)
  • Hugo Award for Best Novel (2001, 2002, 2004, 2008)
  • National Book Awards Children's Book of the Year (1998)
  • Carnegie Medal (1998)
